Geometry

Introduction

Geometry is the study of shapes and their properties.

It is a fundamental area of mathematics that has numerous applications in art, architecture, engineering, and other fields.

Points, Lines, and Planes

A point is a location in space.

A line is a set of points that extend infinitely in two directions.

A plane is a flat surface that extends infinitely in all directions.

Angles and Measurements

An angle is a measure of the amount of rotation between two lines or planes.

Angles can be measured in degrees, radians, or gradians.

For example, the angle between two lines can be measured using a protractor.

Properties of Shapes

A shape is a self-contained area with a defined boundary.

Examples of shapes include triangles, quadrilaterals, polygons, circles, and 3D objects such as pyramids and spheres.

Each shape has its own unique properties, such as the number of sides, the number of angles, and the type of symmetry.
